Operational Heritage

2400 BCE — The Papyrus Checklist

Egyptian scribes used the first recorded checklists to manage the massive logistics of monument construction. This birthed the To-Do: The fundamental human need to document "What" must be done to prevent failure.

1700s — Naval & Military Discipline

Naval units developed Repeatable Onboard Procedures (The first SOPs). In the chaos of the high seas, sailors needed the "How" fused to the "What." This discipline turned groups of men into synchronized, life-saving machines.

1980s — Digital Portability

The first software-based To-Do lists digitized the checklist. For the first time, tasks became bits of data—portable and searchable—but they lost the "Deep Context" of the physical procedures that came before them.

PRESENT — The Hybrid Approach

HexTodo is born from a deep love for focus and stewardship. We re-integrate the 18th-century Naval "How" with the modern Digital "What."
